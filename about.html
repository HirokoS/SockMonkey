Volunteers 2021

Mission:

To make safe, durable, friendly sock monkeys that will ease the child inpatients' fear and anxiety of their medical encounters. But first and foremost, to enjoy sewing!

Vision:

To help our community be aware the value of hand-mades sharing the joy of DIY and to pass down the monkey making tradition and culture to the next generation.

History:

Our Sock Monkey tradition started in February 1962. A group of women began crafting the monkeys to give to children admitted to N.C. Memorial Hospital. Read more

Local Support

UNC Hospital Volunteer Association provides us the socks and sewing supplies. The white stockings we use to stuff monkey's arms and legs are donated from Wells Hosiery Mills Inc. and Bossong Hosiery Mills Inc. Thanks to their support and generosity, we can keep working and making the children happy!

Nidia Sharlock

She dedicated herself to serving child patients at UNC Children's Hospital since 1992. Her services expanded to not only monkey-making but also volunteering for the Ronald McDonald House (RMH) and cap knitting for the Neonatal care unit. She liked to help people and no surprise, her volunteer hours marked the longest ever in the history of the RMH in Chapel Hill. She shared her passion of caring for others with the volunteer group and it's still living in us today. Read her article here.
